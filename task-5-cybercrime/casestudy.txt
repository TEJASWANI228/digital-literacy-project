CYBERCRIME CASE STUDY - UPI PAYMENT FRAUD

What is UPI Fraud?
UPI fraud is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick 
users into sending money or revealing sensitive information 
through the Unified Payments Interface (UPI) system.

How it typically happens (step-by-step):
1. The victim receives a call from someone claiming to be 
   a bank official or customer support representative.
2. The attacker tells the victim their account has an issue 
   and needs immediate verification.
3. The attacker sends a "collect request" on UPI which 
   looks like a "receive money" notification.
4. The victim enters their UPI PIN thinking they are 
   receiving money - but they are actually approving 
   a payment.
5. Money is instantly deducted from the victim's account.
6. The attacker disconnects and becomes unreachable.

Who is usually targeted?
- First-time UPI users
- College students and elderly people
- People who have recently listed items for sale online
- Anyone unfamiliar with how UPI collect requests work

Consequences:
- Immediate financial loss with little chance of recovery
- Personal data exposure leading to further attacks
- Mental stress and loss of trust in digital payments
- In severe cases, victims lose their entire savings

Source: Based on common UPI fraud patterns reported on 
cybercrime.gov.in and CERT-In advisories.
